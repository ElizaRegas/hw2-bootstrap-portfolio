Building the habit of committing via Git is important for two reasons: * Your commit history is a signal to employers that you are actively working on projects and learning new skills. * Your commit history allows you to revert your codebase in the event that you need to return to a previous state. Follow these guidelines for committing: * Make single-purpose commits for related changes to ensure a clean, manageable history.
